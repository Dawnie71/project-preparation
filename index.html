Teens and Alcohol

When you start to feel like you are struggling with alcohol, and you are a teenager, it can be scary, lonely and overwhelming. Teens who question their alcohol intake may not know where to seek help, as raising this issue with the people in your life, who could help, may feel a threat to you. Approaching adults with questions about alcohol use, or issues, is scary for both of you. Parents and teachers know the potential risks to young people and alcohol. These issues can be further complicated if you are a member of an ethnic community or from the LGBTQ community.

Even though it is not legal, or socially acceptible some young people, choose to pick up an alocholic drink. It could be to fit in, appear popular or because of peer pressure. Some people are curious to know what it feels like. Some like how it makes them feel more confident, or it might help them forget difficult thoughts or feelings. Whatever the reason, drinking alcohol doesn’t make you a bad person. But there are laws in place, and alcohol can have a negative effect on your mind and body. So, we’re here to help you know the facts and be aware of the risks.

There are some facts that bring extra concerns when young people drink. Teens who drink put themselves at risk for obvious problems with the law (it's illegal; you can get arrested). Teens who drink are also more likely to get into fights and commit crimes than those who don't. People who drink regularly also often have problems with school. Drinking can damage a student's ability to study, affect grades, increase incidents of disciplinary interventions and affect general attitude and peer groups.
